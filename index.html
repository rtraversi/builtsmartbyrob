Managed Automation Hosting

AI and automation can save your business hours every week — but if you handle confidential information, where that data lives and travels matters as much as what the tools do. Managed Automation Hosting is built for businesses that can't afford to get this wrong.

Every client gets their own private n8n automation environment — either a dedicated container on our managed shared infrastructure, or a fully separate private server owned exclusively by you. Either way, your data is isolated from every other client, connected to AI services under enterprise terms, and never routed through consumer platforms. The result: the same productivity gains you'd get from popular AI tools, with a defensible answer to the question "where does our client data go?"

Why This Matters

Most popular AI and automation tools route your data through shared servers, retain it for years, or use it to train future models. For some businesses that's an inconvenience. For attorneys, healthcare providers, financial advisors, HR teams, and anyone bound by confidentiality obligations, it's a serious liability.

For attorneys specifically: a 2026 federal ruling (US v. Heppner, S.D.N.Y.) confirmed that submitting client information to public AI platforms can be treated as a third-party disclosure — meaning attorney-client privilege can be waived the moment text is pasted into a free AI tool. ABA Model Rules 1.1 (competence), 1.6 (confidentiality), and 5.3 (vendor supervision) apply to every AI tool a firm uses. The architecture you choose determines whether your AI use is defensible.

The same principles apply to anyone with a duty of care over client information — patient records, financial data, HR files, trade secrets. Convenience doesn't excuse exposure.

How Your Setup Stays Separate From Everyone Else's

Unlike mainstream automation platforms that run every customer's data through shared infrastructure, every client on Managed Automation Hosting gets their own isolated environment. We offer two tiers — both are secure, the difference is how deep the separation goes.

On the Shared plan, your workflows run inside their own private container on our managed server. Other clients have containers on the same server, but each container is completely walled off — they can't see your data, access your workflows, or affect how your setup runs in any way.

Think of it like a secure apartment building. Each unit is private — your own locked door, your own space. The neighbors are in the building too, but there's no way into your unit except through your own access. For most businesses, this level of isolation is more than sufficient.

  • Your own isolated container — no shared access with other clients
  • n8n configured and running exclusively for your business
  • Other clients cannot see your data, credentials, or workflows
  • Fully managed, monitored, and maintained on your behalf

On the Dedicated plan, you get your own private server in the cloud — a completely separate machine, set up and maintained exclusively for your business. No other client's data lives on it. No other workflows run on it. At every level of the stack, it belongs to you alone.

Think of it as the difference between a secure apartment and your own private house. Both are safe — but the house is entirely separate property. No shared foundation, no shared infrastructure, no neighbors in the building at all. For businesses with the strictest confidentiality requirements or compliance obligations, this removes any question about shared infrastructure.

  • Your own dedicated private server — zero shared infrastructure
  • A separate n8n instance built and maintained exclusively for you
  • Complete isolation at every level, not just the application layer
  • Ideal for firms with strict compliance, privilege, or regulatory requirements

Running a small business means wearing a lot of hats — and too often, those hats involve repetitive, time-consuming tasks that pull you away from the work that actually grows your business.

That's where automation platforms like Make, Zapier, and n8n come in. These tools connect the apps and services you already use and put them to work together — automatically. Here's what that looks like in the real world:

The Next Level

Pair Automation with AI — and Your Workflows Can Think

Instead of just moving data from point A to point B, your workflows can now reason, interpret, and act on the content itself. This is where the real time savings happen.

  • 📋 Review completed USCIS application packages, cross-check every field against requirements, and flag issues for attorney review — before they go out the door.
  • 📬 When a USCIS notice arrives with a biometric appointment date, read the document, extract the key details, and fire a reminder text to your client automatically — so nothing falls through the cracks.
  • 📧 Incoming client emails can be analyzed, categorized, and prioritized before a human even reads them — turning a flooded inbox into a managed queue.

These are exactly the kinds of tasks that eat up hours of staff time every week — and exactly the kinds of tasks that automation handles better, faster, and more consistently than any manual process can.
